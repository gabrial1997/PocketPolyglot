# v0.1.2 ‚Äî "Round 1: see == hear" fidelity pass

> Design doc / spec. Status: **approved 2026-06-18**, ready for an implementation plan.
> Authored from a brainstorming session on three founder-reported issues ("round 1").
> Read `CLAUDE.md` (the non-negotiable card boundary + locked product constraints) and
> `handoverv0.1.0.md` (current build state) alongside this.

## Goal

Every card plays the **right** clip for what it shows. The word the learner **sees** is always
the same word they **hear** ‚Äî the *see == hear invariant*. Fix the three reported screens **and**
the root cause of each, so the whole drill / diphthong / say-it family is correct, not just the
three screens in the bug reports.

This is the **correctness/fidelity** framing the founder chose ‚Äî not a minimal one-line patch.
Ships as v0.1.2: **app code + re-seeded Supabase content together**, verified end-to-end on a
real iPhone in light + dark.

### The invariant (the spine of this work)

> **On any perception or pronunciation card, the word displayed and the word played are
> identical.** Translation-recall cards (e.g. the phrase "say it" card, which cues in English and
> asks the learner to *produce* the Latvian from memory) are the one deliberate exception ‚Äî there,
> seeing English while producing/hearing Latvian *is* the exercise.

## Background ‚Äî why these are real bugs

Confirmed at the code level + by inspecting the live DB and downloading the audio objects:

- **Issue 1 ‚Äî `ie` glide plays "lieta".** The diphthong drill's data carries only the
  contrast-word clips (`drill-lieta.mp3` = the whole word "lieta"). There is **no isolated-glide
  audio anywhere** in the pipeline. The "Meet the glide" intro step (`DiphthongDrillScreen`) plays
  `item.audio.nativeUrl` = the whole word. The card meant to teach the glide in isolation has
  nothing isolated to play.
- **Issue 2 ‚Äî drill "say it back" shows `lƒÅcis`, plays a different word.** `DrillScreen` hardcodes
  the say-it word to `item.target` = `row.a` ("lƒÅcis"), but the play orb plays
  `item.audio.nativeUrl` = the **stimulus** clip (`drill-loti.mp3`). So the screen shows the
  word that was *not* played, and plays the stimulus (the correct answer's word). Two unrelated
  words on one screen ‚Üí violates see == hear. Additionally, the per-option clips
  (`drill-loti-a` = "lƒÅcis", `drill-loti-b` = "ƒºoti") are uploaded to Storage by the seeder but
  **never written to the DB and never mapped into the `ReviewItem`**, so no per-side playback is
  even possible. (The six drill clips were downloaded and are all distinct files ‚Äî this is not a
  "two rows share one file" bug; the founder reported hearing "lieta", which `drill-loti.mp3` is
  not, so a clip-content verification is included below.)
- **Issue 3 ‚Äî phrase card not centered + say-it cue.** `CardShell` sets `justifyContent: 'center'`
  (vertical) but **no `alignItems`/`textAlign` center**, so every CardShell-based card renders
  **left-aligned** ‚Äî play orb and mic hug the left edge with dead space below. The mockups
  (`mockups/phrase_cards.png`) are fully centered. Separately, `PhraseSayIt` shows only the
  English gloss with a play orb that lets the learner hear the answer before recording (a recall
  leak).

## Non-negotiable constraints (must hold through every change)

From `CLAUDE.md`:

- **Cards are pure: data-in (`ReviewItem`) / events-out (`CardResult`).** Fixes land as item
  fields + theme tokens + card-local UI state + at most one new mapper field. No card imports a
  service; no hardcoded content.
- **Theme tokens only; light + dark both verified** for every visual change.
- **`CardKind` `id`/`k` strings stay stable.**
- **Wrong-answer rule untouched** (red "Not quite ‚Äî give it another try.", no reveal, no advance).
- **No gamification.** The unlock chime stays the only celebratory beat.
- Each **live migration needs its own explicit user authorization** (the permission classifier
  gates them individually).

## Design

### Issue 1 ‚Äî isolated `ie` glide (recorded, stubbed now)

**Data model.** Add `glide_audio_url text` to `minimal_pairs` (migration **`0007`**). Populated
only for diphthong drills; `null` for plain consonant pairs.

**Content.** The "Meet the glide" step needs a clip of the **isolated glide**, not the word.
Decision: the real clip will be **recorded** (the first real recording in the app ‚Äî candidate for
Elizabete's voice). For v0.1.2 we ship a **placeholder** glide clip at a known asset path
(`content-pipeline/assets/glide-ie.mp3`), clearly marked temporary. The plan picks the simplest
stub available in the build environment ‚Äî preferred: a short "ie" segment trimmed from the
existing `drill-lieta` render (most faithful); acceptable fallback: a brief TTS of the glide. The
stub exists to make the wiring verifiable on the phone, not to be the final sound.
**Swap path:** drop the real recording at that asset path and re-run the seeder ‚Äî **no code
change**.

**App.** Mapper carries `glide.audioUrl` (extend the existing `ReviewGlide` shape /
`item.glide`). `DiphthongDrillScreen`'s "Meet the glide" intro step plays `item.glide.audioUrl`
when present (falling back to the word clip only if it's missing). The later "hear it in a word"
and contrast steps continue to play the word clips. The on-screen "ie" hero + "tap to hear the
glide" copy already match the mockup ‚Äî only the *audio source* for that one tap changes.

### Issue 2 ‚Äî drill say-it-back: see == hear

**App (the core fix).** In `DrillScreen`, the say-it-back word **is the stimulus word** ‚Äî the word
that was played and that the learner correctly identified ‚Äî **shown and played identically**:

- The say-it label = the stimulus word (the correct side's word), not `row.a`.
- The play orb on the say-it step plays the stimulus clip (same word).
- Result: see == hear by construction, and the pedagogy is corrected (today it asks the learner
  to say back the word that was *not* played).

**Root-cause close ‚Äî per-option audio.** Extend the pair data so each side has its own clip:

- Add `a_audio_url`, `b_audio_url` to `minimal_pairs` (part of migration **`0007`**), populated
  from the already-uploaded `<slug>-a` / `<slug>-b` Storage objects (seeder change: write the URLs
  it currently discards).
- Map them into `item.pair` (`aAudioUrl`, `bAudioUrl`).
- Any per-side playback then plays the correct word. (Tapping a choice button currently plays
  nothing; this design keeps choice-buttons silent unless a later UX decision adds per-side
  playback ‚Äî but the *data* is now correct and available, closing the root gap.)

**Verification task (not an assumption).** Transcribe/verify `golden/drill-loti.mp3` actually
says "ƒºoti". The founder reported hearing "lieta" on the `lƒÅcis` screen; the code path plays
`drill-loti.mp3`. If the stored clip's content is wrong (bad TTS input), regenerate it. The
see == hear fix is required regardless of the outcome.

### Issue 3 ‚Äî phrase card layout + say-it cue

**Layout.** Fix `CardShell` to center horizontally: add `alignItems: 'center'` to the body and
`textAlign: 'center'` to the text styles, matching every mockup. This intentionally affects **all**
CardShell-based cards (they are all meant to be centered). Snapshots will change ‚Äî review and
update them intentionally (`jest -u`), do not blind-accept.

**Phrase say-it cue (founder's choice: English-only, no pre-hear).** Rework `PhraseSayIt`:

- **Cue stage:** show the **English** prompt only; **no play orb** (no hearing the answer before
  producing it). A single mic to record.
- **After recording:** show the **compare** ‚Äî *hear native* vs *hear you* ‚Äî wiring `onPlayCompare`
  (which already exists in `cardWiring` but `PhraseSayIt` does not currently use). Then the
  self-rate Good/Again.
- This is true productive recall and keeps the see == hear exception explicit and intentional.

### Issue 4 ‚Äî regression: "Begin session" doesn't reach the cards

**Diagnosis (not a code bug).** `SessionHost` (`src/navigation/index.tsx`) bounces straight back to
home when the due batch is empty (`finished = done || !current` ‚Üí `onExit()`). `getDueBatch`
returns rows where `due_at <= now OR stage = 'new'`. The live test-user deck has been **walked
through**: all 12 `review_state` rows are now `stage = 'review'` with future `due_at` (only ~1 due,
0 `new`). So the batch is ~empty and the session bounces. The v0.1.1 audit did not cause this
(its `mappers.ts` change is in `schedule()`, post-review, not `getDueBatch`); the audit/testing
session simply played the deck. The 184 tests stayed green because they use fixtures, not live
data.

**Fix (founder's choice: re-seed only).** Re-running the golden-slice seeder wipes + re-inserts the
engineered `review_state` (restoring the `stage:'new'` items and due reviews) ‚Äî the deck is pristine
again and "Begin session" enters the cards. This is **already covered** by the re-seed step in the
content/delivery section; no app-code change. **Accepted known limitation:** the silent bounce on a
genuinely empty deck remains (no "all caught up" screen this round) ‚Äî revisit post-v0.1.2 if it
bites.

### Issue 5 ‚Äî "Glide Left" card transition

**What.** Incorporate the prototype's **Glide Left** card-to-card motion (handoff:
`handover/design_handoff_glide_transition/`). Advancing eases the current card left (translateX
`0 ‚Üí -32%`, scale `1 ‚Üí 0.93`, opacity `1 ‚Üí 0`) while the next glides in from the right (translateX
`+34% ‚Üí 0`, scale `0.97 ‚Üí 1`, opacity `0 ‚Üí 1`), over **600 ms**. Move easing
`cubic-bezier(.16,1,.3,1)` (easeOutExpo); opacity runs ~80% of the duration (‚âà480 ms); incoming
card sits above with 36 px corners + a soft drop shadow (light `0 26px 56px rgba(26,39,51,0.22)`,
dark `0 26px 56px rgba(0,0,0,0.55)`).

**Where (boundary-safe).** Host-level plumbing only ‚Äî a new `GlideViewport` + `useGlide` state
machine wrapping the card in `SessionHost`. **Pure cards are untouched**; they don't know they're
being transitioned.

**RN port decisions.**
- **Library:** React Native's built-in `Animated` (already used by `Waveform`/`GlideTrack`) ‚Äî **no
  new dependency.** translateX uses screen-width-relative values (no `%` transforms in RN), scale +
  opacity are `Animated.Value`s, easing via `Easing.bezier(0.16,1,0.3,1)`.
- **Blur: omitted (founder's choice).** RN has no animated `filter: blur` without adding
  `expo-blur`, and animating a `BlurView` is janky; the position/scale/opacity carry the feel. This
  is the one deliberate fidelity compromise.
- **Mechanism:** keep the outgoing item rendered alongside the incoming one for the transition
  duration (capture `prevItem`), animate both layers, then drop the outgoing card. The existing
  `key={item.id}` remount stays for the *committed* card so per-card ephemeral state never leaks.
- **Reduced motion:** honor `AccessibilityInfo.isReduceMotionEnabled()` ‚Üí skip transform, use a
  short (‚â§120 ms) opacity fade or cut.
- **Forward-only this round:** the deck advances forward; the back/prev mirror is documented in the
  handoff but not wired (no back navigation in the session flow yet).

### Content / delivery

Two delivery channels, coordinated in one cycle:

- **App code** (CardShell, mappers, `DrillScreen`, `DiphthongDrillScreen`, `PhraseSayIt`,
  types) ‚Üí ships in the JS bundle / app build.
- **Content** (the placeholder glide clip; `glide_audio_url` + `a_audio_url`/`b_audio_url`
  columns and values; any regenerated drill clip) ‚Üí ships via Supabase: migration `0007` applied
  live (with explicit authorization), then the golden-slice seeder re-run to upload clips and
  refresh rows. This is best practice for a content-heavy app ‚Äî content updates without an App
  Store release.

## Testing & acceptance

**Automated gate (must stay green):** `npx tsc --noEmit && npx eslint . && npx jest`.
- New/updated unit tests: mapper carries `glide.audioUrl` + `pair.aAudioUrl/bAudioUrl`;
  `DrillScreen` say-it target == stimulus word; `PhraseSayIt` cue stage has no play orb and the
  compare stage exposes native/you playback.
- Snapshots updated intentionally for the centered `CardShell` and the reworked `PhraseSayIt`.

**Live phone walk (light + dark):**
1. Diphthong drill ‚Üí "Meet the glide" plays an isolated glide (placeholder), **not** "lieta".
2. L/ƒª drill ‚Üí say-it-back screen: the word shown == the word the play orb plays (see == hear).
3. Phrase say-it ‚Üí English cue, no audio until after recording, then native-vs-you compare.
4. Phrase/word cards are **centered** and match the mockup composition.
5. After re-seed, "Begin session" **enters the cards** (deck no longer empty).
6. Advancing between cards plays the **Glide Left** transition (forward), smooth on-device, and
   degrades to a fade under reduced-motion.

**Acceptance:** all four above pass in both themes; the gate is green; the live golden-slice deck
is restored to a pristine state by the seeder.

## Out of scope (carried, not addressed here)

  re-seed, no code change.
- Adding tap-to-play on the drill choice buttons (data is made available; UX decision deferred).
- The broader content track (top-1000 native sign-off, full TTS corpus, ElevenLabs voice).
- Any other mockup screens not implicated by these issues.
- An "all caught up" empty-deck state (founder chose re-seed only this round; silent bounce stays).
- The back/prev mirror of the Glide transition (no back navigation in the session flow yet).
- Animated blur on the Glide transition (RN limitation; omitted by choice).
